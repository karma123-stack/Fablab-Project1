3D Printer (FDM)

FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) 3D printers are one of the most common types of 3D printers used today. They work by extruding melted thermoplastic filament through a heated nozzle to build objects layer by layer.

The CST FabLab utilizes FDM printers for rapid prototyping and educational projects. Materials used include PLA, ABS, and PETG, which are versatile and user-friendly. These printers are ideal for students and hobbyists looking to create functional prototypes, custom parts, or artistic models.

Our team guides beginners in learning how to prepare 3D models, slice them using software like Cura or PrusaSlicer, and safely operate the printers. We ensure proper training so users can make the most out of their designs and understand the underlying fabrication process.
